Hint: The sequence of numbers in each space has a specific pattern, broken into two parts. Can you find how each sequence is structured?

Solution: To find the solution, you must combine two specific patterns into the final result. The first is to find out which circles are touching a particular space. If you order the numbers inside the circles based on the size of the circles, smallest to largest, you will get the first part of the sequence. For example, with the leftmost middle space, the circle with the "7" is the smallest, then the circle with the "9", and finally the circle with the "4" is the largest. Therefore, "794" is the first part of the sequence. For the last number, we just need to find how many circles the space is touching. In this case, the space is touching three circles, so the last number is a "3". Combining the two parts, we get "7943". Applying this logic to the unknown space, we get the sequence "856165".

Mon, August 17th, 2020

If you want to download the pdf version of the puzzle, click here.

Show Hint Show Solution

Hint: The arrows refer to the orientation of each letter. Can you separate the letters into groups based on their orientation?

Solution: To create a full message, we need to combine the letters in order of their orientation. First, we take the letters indicated by the up arrow, with a normal orientation. Those letters in order, while trying to intutively separate them into words, gives us "I AM WHITE WHEN". Then, if we take the letters that have been rotated to the left, we get "I AM DIRTY AND BLA". Continuing with the order, we get "CK WHEN I AM CLEAN WH" when taking the letters rotated to the right. Finally, we get "AT AM I" taking the upside down letters. Combining all four parts, we get the message, "I AM WHITE WHEN I AM DIRTY AND BLACK WHEN I AM CLEAN WHAT AM I". In a more readable format, it is the riddle, "I am white when I am dirty and black when I am clean. What am I?" The answer to this riddle, and the whole puzzle, is blackboard / chalkboard.
